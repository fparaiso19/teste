The SLT Therapeutic Paradox

Understanding the mechanism creates a clinical dilemma

🔬

SLT Mechanism

Selective photolysis triggers inflammatory cascade (IL-1α, IL-1β, IL-8, TNF-α) recruiting macrophages to remodel trabecular meshwork

📈

Therapeutic Effect

Inflammation-mediated debris clearance and extracellular matrix remodeling reduces outflow resistance

⚡

Adverse Effects

Same inflammation causes post-procedural pain, anterior chamber reaction, and acute IOP spikes

💊

Current Practice

NSAIDs and corticosteroids routinely prescribed, but no consensus on optimal agent

⚠️

The Paradox

By treating post-SLT symptoms with anti-inflammatory agents, are we inadvertently suppressing the therapeutic effect of the laser itself?

Research Objective

To compare the effects of different topical anti-inflammatory agents following SLT and establish a ranked treatment hierarchy based on clinical outcomes

Primary Outcome
IOP change at 1 month
Secondary Outcomes
IOP spikes, Pain, AC inflammation

Conclusions

1
Diclofenac 0.1% appears most effective for optimizing IOP outcomes following SLT, with consistent top rankings and statistically significant benefit in common effects analysis
2
Dexamethasone 0.1% consistently demonstrated the least favorable IOP outcomes, performing numerically worse than placebo across all time points
3
Treatment selection involves trade-offs: agents ranking highly for IOP (indomethacin) ranked poorly for pain, and vice versa (ketorolac)
4
Evidence certainty is low to very low — larger, adequately powered head-to-head trials are needed to confirm these hierarchies
